Peer-to-Peer Massage

Our Peer-to-Peer Massage programme is aimed specifically at children aged 4-13 years.

The format is simple and well-structured; it has been developed as an inclusive whole school approach.

The clothed, peer massage routines encourages positive relationships through massage which is given and received with the child's permission. The children perform the back, neck and shoulder massages on each other for 10-15 minutes each day.

The massage routine is taught in two parts, so the class can practise the techniques in between sessions and we can check that the techniques are being performed correctly.

In Reception, Year 1 and Year 2, the routine uses the story of The Gruffalo to help the children better engage with the techniques and have fun along the way.

Some of the benefits are described below:

  • Improves relationships, social skills and cooperation amongst children as they work in pairs or groups, encouraging them to learn from each other
  • Teaches children to know the difference between positive, healthy touch and negative, inappropriate behaviour
  • Encourages children to make choices
  • Reduces stress, aggression and bullying
  • Teaches respect and reinforces inclusion
  • Helps improve self-esteem
  • Provides kinaesthetic and visual approach to learning
  • Improves concentration and helps make the classroom a calmer, conducive working environment
  • Links to curriculum areas such as Every Child Matters, PHSCE, SEAL, SEN, Learning and behaviour support
  • Embraces diversity
